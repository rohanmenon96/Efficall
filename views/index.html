How does it work?

What is contextualization?

After getting a summary, we may further extract important information from the input text. This information may include important meeting dates, scheduled appointments and future commitments. Our algorithm traces key trigger-words from the conversation and gives out bullet points like "To-Do" items to the user.

So how do we optimize?

As every phone call can be characterized by two parameters - Quality and Cost - we use these two metrics to judge our available connections.

Let's consider we have 3 active connections - WiFi , Mobile-Data and Cellular Network - to make a phone call. Each of these connections will be given a Quality and Cost rating.

For Example, WiFi would have a Wc (WiFi Cost Rating) and WQ (WiFi quality rating). We would then find a Efficiency Factor (WQ/Wc) - which would be a ratio between the quality and cost rating. We would then do it for all the other connections. Finally, we would suggest the connection which would have the highest Efficiency factor.

So how do we summarise?

After obtaining a recorded conversation , either through a recorded audio file or a text file, we use Natual Language processing to obtain a meaningful summary. We use an algorithm similar to the famed PageRank Algorithm to along with NLP to obtain this result.
